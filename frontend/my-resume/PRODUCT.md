# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

**Primary**: Recruiters and hiring managers evaluating Raghav Ahuja's technical and creative capabilities. They arrive with a resume in hand and leave with a conviction — this engineer builds things that are both well-architected and beautifully designed.

**Secondary**: Friends and peers in the CS/engineering community who want to see what's possible when you treat a portfolio as a craft project, not a checkbox.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio that proves the claim "I am both technically and creatively good" — not by stating it, but by demonstrating it through every pixel. The site is itself a work sample: the infrastructure animation, the polished UI, the dark/light theme, the responsive layout, and the cloud-resume-challenge backend all serve as live evidence.

Success means a recruiter finishes browsing and thinks "I need to talk to this person" — not because the resume says the right keywords, but because the site itself communicates competence and taste.

## Positioning

A portfolio that proves its owner is both technically and creatively exceptional — the site itself is the strongest work sample.

## Brand Personality

**Modern · Intriguing · Clean**

The tone is confident but not loud — Apple's restrained precision, Notion's understated clarity, and Linear's purposeful minimalism. The site should feel thoughtfully edited: nothing excessive, nothing missing. Every element earns its place.

The infra animation (traveling data packets through an architecture diagram) is the signature differentiator — worth evolving as a core brand element.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI-generated templates**: The terminal-with-green-text motif, identical card grids with placeholder images, glassmorphism, gradient text, and the "AI color palette" (cyan-on-dark, purple-to-blue gradients) are all banned.
- **The SaaS-cream default**: Warm beige/sand backgrounds tinted toward warmth-without-reason. If the background has color, it should be intentional and tied to the brand.
- **Over-designed complexity**: Not every element needs a shadow, a border-radius, and a hover lift. Edit ruthlessly.

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the proof.** Every design decision should make the visitor more convinced of the owner's technical and creative ability. If a choice doesn't serve that, remove it.
2. **Restraint over decoration.** Apple, Notion, and Linear are references because they do more with less. Use whitespace, typography, and hierarchy — not effects — to create impact.
3. **One memorable thing.** The infra animation is that thing. Protect it, evolve it, and don't compete with it.
4. **Edit, don't add.** When in doubt, remove rather than layer on. A portfolio with fewer, better elements is more convincing than one with everything.
5. **Consistency is credibility.** The same spacing, color tokens, typography, and interaction patterns everywhere. Nothing should feel like it belongs to a different site.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA compliance target (4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text, keyboard-navigable, focus indicators).
- `prefers-reduced-motion` respected — animations enhance but never gate content.
- Semantic HTML and proper heading hierarchy throughout.
- Dark mode with maintained contrast ratios.
